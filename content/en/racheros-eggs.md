## Ingredients (2 servings)
- 4 eggs  
- 4 corn tortillas  
- 1 tbsp oil (plus extra for tortillas)  
- 1 onion, diced  
- 2 garlic cloves, minced  
- 2 tomatoes, diced (or 200g canned chopped tomatoes)  
- 1 jalapeño, finely chopped  
- ½ tsp paprika  
- ½ tsp cumin  
- ½ tsp dried oregano  
- Salt & pepper to taste  
- Optional: coriander, lime, cheese, avocado  

---

## Meal Prep Steps
1. Dice the onion, tomatoes, and jalapeño.  
2. Mince the garlic.  
3. Warm tortillas by lightly frying or toasting them and keep warm.  
4. Crack eggs into a bowl if you want to fry them quickly later.

---

## Cooking Instructions

### Make the Ranchero Sauce
1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a saucepan on medium heat.  
2. Add onion and cook 3–4 minutes until softened.  
3. Add garlic and jalapeño; cook 1 minute.  
4. Stir in tomatoes, paprika, cumin, and oregano.  
5. Season with salt and pepper.  
6. Simmer 8–10 minutes until thickened. Adjust seasoning.

### Cook the Eggs
1. Heat a lightly oiled frying pan over medium heat.  
2. Crack in the eggs and fry until whites are set but yolks are still runny (or to preferred doneness).  
3. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.

### Assemble
1. Place warm tortillas on plates.  
2. Spoon ranchero sauce over each.  
3. Top with a fried egg.  
4. Add coriander, cheese, lime, or avocado if desired.

---

## Storage & Reheating Instructions
- **Sauce:** Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in a pan over low heat.  
- **Eggs:** Best cooked fresh; avoid storing.  
- **Tortillas:** Keep wrapped and warm just before serving; can be reheated in a dry pan or microwave.
